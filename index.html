Exhibit 1: First Frame

Thomas Edison's contribution to film is often underrated but never underestimated. By creating the electric light bulb, he fixed film's then biggest problem: Natural Lighting. Before the bulb, movies had to rely on the weather to get the shot they wanted, often resorting in long breaks in between sets due to a lack of control over the environment.
