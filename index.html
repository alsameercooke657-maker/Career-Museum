WHAT IS... The Silent Film Era?

Lasting from the mid-1890’s to the late 1920’s, the silent film era was a transformative period in cinema history, characterized by films without synchronized film dialogue. Rather, these movies relied on visual storytelling, exaggerated acting and intertitles accompanied by live music. The films shown here are key figures in not only representing the time period of cinema, but for cinema as a whole.
