WHAT IS... The Silent Film Era?

Lasting from the mid-1890’s to the late 1920’s, the silent film era was a transformative period in cinema history, characterized by films without synchronized film dialogue. Rather, these movies relied on visual storytelling, exaggerated acting and intertitles accompanied by live music. The films shown here are key figures in not only representing the time period of cinema, but for cinema as a whole.

KEY FIGURES

The Birth of a Nation

A landmark in early filmmaking known for its technical innovation in editing and scale, while also being widely criticized for its racist themes.

Metropolis

A futuristic silent epic exploring class struggle in a massive industrial city, famous for its expressionist visuals and design.

Battleship Potemkin

A Soviet montage masterpiece known for its emotional intensity and the iconic Odessa Steps sequence.

The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari

A defining German Expressionist film with distorted visuals and a psychological, twisted narrative.

The General

A Buster Keaton classic blending comedy and action, celebrated for its physical stunt work and precision filmmaking.
